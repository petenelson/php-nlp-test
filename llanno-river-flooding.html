A week after a deadly flood swept through a Llano RV park, the waters along the South Llano River are again rapidly rising.

A flash flood warning is in effect for Llano and Gillespie counties. About 5:45 a.m. Tuesday radar indicated light to moderate rainfall would continue across those counties, according to the National Weather Service.

As much as 10 inches of rain has fallen on saturated ground during the past 30 hours, leading to multiple road closures because of high water.

The Llano River is was expected to crest at 41 feet, warns meteorlogists.

"This is a very dangerous situation for people living within vulnerable areas along the Llano River. Seek higher ground immediately," the National Weather Service warned. Locations that will experience flooding include: Fredericksburg, Kingsland, Llano, Buchanan Dam, Gainesville, Horseshoe Bay, Buchanan Lake Village, Castell, Field Creek, Tivydale, Bluffton, Baby Head, Stolz, Crabapple, Oxford, Cherry Spring, Lone Grove, Enchanted Rock State Natural Area, Doss and Willow City.

A week ago the Llano River poured out of its banks and swept away residents of an RV park. Four people were reported missing and three bodies have been recovered during search operations. Those operations are suspended, according to news reports from Llano this morning.

“The situation here is not as bad as it sounded,” said Kimble County Emergency Cooridnator Randy Millican.

Around 2 a.m. Millican went around to residents living in low-lying areas to warn them of rising water in the tributaries. This areas normally are just a small creek, but with the river so high, water is filling in them, he said.

At 6:10 a.m. the Johnson Fork overflowed over the I-10 and the authorities temporarily shut it down. They rescued three people from a vehicle that was trapped in the water.

On Monday, the search for the last missing person continued. The searchers utilized the maps created by Texas Search and Rescue to locate debris fields to search.

“We are dealing with the events that unfolded this morning, and will resume the search once it’s contained,” said Millican.

The people reported missing were San Angelo resident Darin Hartman and Flomaton, Alabama, residents Mike McGee, Charlotte Moye and Joey Huss. Officials have not released the identities of those already found.

During the flooding Monday, Oct. 8, 2018 the Llano River peaked at 33 feet, 2 feet above major flood stage.

This time the river is expected to reach 41 feet, officials said.
